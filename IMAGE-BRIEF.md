# Syntropy Earth — Image Brief
## What to source, where it goes, and how to prepare it

The site is built to look finished with zero photos, on typography, colour, and motion. Images are an enhancement, not a dependency. Add them when ready. Everything below is free for commercial use from Pexels or Unsplash.

**Golden rule from your style guide:** documentary and human, warm, slightly underexposed. Real situations, not stock-smiling. No polar bears, no melting glaciers, no sad-child clichés. Human consequence and working reality over environmental spectacle.

---

## Priority 1 — The founder photo (you're providing this)

- **Where:** Homepage Founder section, and optionally the About page hero area.
- **Spec:** portrait orientation, at least 800 x 1000px. A real, warm, considered photo. Not a stiff corporate headshot. Natural light, ideally an environment that reads thoughtful rather than salesy.
- **How to add:** save as `images/abhijith-magal.jpg` in the project. In `index.html`, find the Founder section comment and replace the placeholder block with the `<img>` line that's written there ready to uncomment.
- **Optimise first:** resize to about 800px wide, export as JPG at 80% quality, aim for under 150KB. Use squoosh.app (free, drag-and-drop) if you don't have an editor.

## Priority 2 — The social share image (og-image.png)

This is the picture that appears when the site is shared on LinkedIn or WhatsApp. Until it exists, shares show text only.

- **Spec:** exactly 1200 x 630px, PNG.
- **Simplest version:** a deep-forest background (#1E4435) with the headline "Climate change is a healthcare story." in Cormorant Garamond cream, the syntropy mark, and "Syntropy Earth" small. You can make this in Canva in ten minutes (set a custom 1200x630 canvas). No photo needed.
- **Where it goes:** save as `og-image.png` in the project root. It's already referenced in every page's head.

## How to prepare any image (do this every time)

1. **Resize** to the largest size it will display at (1600px wide for full-width, 800px for the portrait). Never upload a 4000px original.
2. **Convert to WebP** (squoosh.app does this). WebP is far smaller than JPG or PNG at the same quality. The portrait founder photo can stay JPG if easier.
3. **Compress** to under 200KB for landscape, under 150KB for the portrait.
4. **Name it descriptively:** `india-heat-construction-worker.webp`, not `IMG_4821.jpg`. Filenames are a small SEO signal.
5. **Always write alt text** when you place it: a plain description of what's in the image. This is required for accessibility and helps SEO. Example: `alt="Construction workers on an Indian site during peak summer heat"`.

---

## Where to download (both free, both commercial-use)

- **Unsplash** (unsplash.com): higher-end, more editorial, fewer results for India-specific health scenes.
- **Pexels** (pexels.com): broader India coverage, good for clinics, pharmacies, workers, monsoon.

Attribution is not legally required on either, but crediting the photographer is a decent thing to do where you can.

---

## What not to do

- No generic "global business handshake" or "doctor pointing at hologram" stock. It instantly cheapens the brand.
- No heavily filtered or oversaturated images. Your palette is warm and restrained; images should match.
- No images of identifiable patients in vulnerable situations. Dignity first, always, and it protects you legally.
