## 1. What this is

The Syntropy Guide is a free, public resource library that lives inside syntropyearth.com. It is the plain-English reference layer underneath everything Syntropy Earth builds, the glossary, the frameworks, the regulatory context, the templates, separated from the paid advisory work and given away without a login wall or an email gate.

It exists to do three things at once:

1. **Give Google and AI answer engines something to find and cite before anyone ever talks to you.** Right now, every proof point of your thinking lives inside a proposal, a strategy document, or a call. None of it is crawlable. The Guide fixes that.
2. **Turn every LinkedIn post, newsletter issue, and workshop into a link, not a dead end.** Content currently disappears into the feed after 48 hours. A resource library gives it a permanent home to point back to.
3. **Build the first layer of the data-capture moat your own strategy documents already flag as missing.** A structured glossary and evidence library, built consistently over time, is the beginning of a proprietary climate-health data asset, not just marketing collateral.

This document is the full map of what the Guide could become. Section 9 tells you what's actually built and sitting in this folder right now, versus what's roadmap only.

## 2. Why "Syntropy Guide" is the right name

It passes the brand's own removal test: strip the name off and it wouldn't read as belonging to anyone else. "Resources," "Learning Hub," and "Knowledge Base" are the names every consultancy site defaults to, they signal nothing. "Syntropy Guide" reads as a proper noun you can build a URL path around (`syntropyearth.com/guide`), reference by name in a LinkedIn post ("new entry in the Guide"), and eventually spin into its own newsletter section without confusing anyone about what it is.

Suggested descriptor line for the site: **"The plain-English reference behind our advisory work."** This is more honest and more differentiated than "your guide to sustainability at work", it tells the reader this isn't generic ESG content, it's the working material of a specific practice, given away.

## 3. Full resource taxonomy

Your original nine categories were the right instinct. Here is the expanded map, what you named, plus what a resource library at this intersection genuinely needs to become an authority asset rather than a content dump.

### 3.1 The Glossary
Plain-English, one-paragraph definitions for every term a non-specialist hits in a sustainability report, CSR meeting, or climate risk conversation. This is the highest-leverage category for SEO and AI answer engines, because glossary definitions are exactly the shape of content that gets lifted into a featured snippet or an AI Overview answer, short, factual, sourced, unambiguous.

*Built in this pass:* 12 sample entries (BRSR, CSR/Section 135, TCFD, GRI, ESRS, Net Zero, Scope 1/2/3, Materiality Assessment, Duty of Care, Adaptation, WBGT).

### 3.2 Regulations & Standards Library
Decoded and compared: BRSR, GRI, TCFD, ESRS, SDGs, and India-specific instruments (NAAQS, MoEFCC notifications, state heat action plans). The comparison-table format is the single most linkable content shape in this category, "BRSR vs GRI" is a query pattern people actually type.

*Built in this pass:* one comparison page (five major frameworks).

### 3.3 Role-Based Guides
What climate-health risk means for a specific seat, not a generic reader: CSR Manager, Sustainability/ESG Lead, HR/Duty-of-Care Owner, CFO, Operations/Supply Chain Head, Pharma Access Programme Lead. Each guide should answer the same five questions in that role's language: what lands on your desk, where it fits in what you already own, the argument that gets budget approved, the objections you'll hear, and what to ask for first.

*Built in this pass:* one full guide (CSR Manager) plus the hub structure for the rest.

### 3.4 Business Decision Guides
Structured "should we / how do we" answers to recurring calls: budgeting a CSR programme against Schedule VII, choosing between reporting frameworks, briefing a board on climate exposure, deciding whether a climate-health diagnostic is worth commissioning. Best format: decision tree or scored checklist, not prose, these pages exist to shorten a decision, not to be read end to end.

*Not built this pass, Phase 2.*

### 3.5 Sector Playbooks
One page per priority segment, mapped directly to your outreach territory: pharma, FMCG, manufacturing, IT, financial services, cement and metals, telecom, retail. Each playbook states where climate-health exposure actually shows up in that sector's operations, and which of the four budget doors (CSR, HQ mandate, duty of care, BRSR) is most likely already open there.

*Not built this pass, Phase 2, pharma first since it's your deepest domain.*

### 3.6 Climate-Health & Equity Library
The evidence base connecting climate change to health outcomes, heat, air quality, food security, healthcare access disruption, written so it can be cited in someone else's reporting, with sources named. This is where your climate-justice-health-equity positioning gets a permanent, linkable home instead of living only in pitch decks.

*Not built this pass, Phase 2/3.*

### 3.7 India Policy & Deadline Tracker
The calendar version of the budget-attachment logic your entire GTM is built on: BRSR filing windows, CSR spend deadlines, notification updates, COP dates, state heat action plan release cycles. This page should be the one you personally check weekly, because every entry in it is a live pitch trigger, not just content.

*Not built this pass, Phase 3.*

### 3.8 Reading List & Tool Directory
Reports, courses, and external tools worth someone's time, with a one-line verdict on each rather than a link dump. Curated lists earn backlinks because they save the reader research time, this is a cheap authority-building category once the rest of the Guide exists to anchor it.

*Not built this pass, Phase 3.*

### 3.9 Templates & Tools
Downloadable trackers, checklists, and self-assessments. No email gate, frictionless downloads with UTM tracking are more consistent with the brand's "credible and calm" register than a lead-gen form, and they still tell you, via analytics, who's downloading what.

*Built in this pass:* one full working template (Climate-Health Materiality Tracker, .xlsx) plus a hub listing five more planned.

### 3.10 FAQ / Mythbuster Hub, new category
Short, direct answers to the questions people actually ask, written to be liftable whole by an AI answer engine: "Is BRSR mandatory for my company?" "Does CSR spend have to be a donation?" "Can climate risk work count toward Section 135?" This category exists specifically for AEO/GEO capture, it's the fastest-growing surface for how people find expert answers now, and almost nobody in the climate-health space is building for it deliberately.

*Not built this pass, Phase 2, high priority.*

### 3.11 Comparison & "X vs Y" Pages, new category
Standalone versions of comparisons that don't fit neatly in the Regulations Library: CSR vs philanthropy, mitigation vs adaptation, BRSR Core vs full BRSR, health equity vs health access. These are deliberately narrow, single-question pages, they rank well precisely because they don't try to cover everything.

*Not built this pass, Phase 2.*

### 3.12 Case Study & Pattern Library, new category
Not client case studies (those stay proprietary and go in the proposal, not the public site), anonymised *pattern* write-ups: "what a climate-health disruption to an access programme typically looks like in practice," built from public reporting and known event patterns, not from confidential client data. This is where public education and proprietary IP stay firmly separated: patterns are public, your specific scoring methodology never is.

*Not built this pass, Phase 3.*

### 3.13 Calculators & Interactive Tools, new category
Simple, browser-based tools that do a small calculation and hand back a number: a CSR-budget-to-CAST-scope estimator, a heat-days-cost-of-downtime calculator, a sector climate-exposure quick-check. These convert better than any static page because they ask the visitor to participate, and they're the natural bridge from "browsing the Guide" to "talking to Syntropy Earth."

*Not built this pass, Phase 3. Needs its own lightweight build once the static content proves the traffic is worth it.*

### 3.14 Downloadable Templates, full list to build toward
Beyond the materiality tracker already built: BRSR Core readiness self-assessment, CSR budget-to-Schedule VII mapper, climate risk register template, access programme resilience checklist (quick-scan version of the four CAST dimensions), heat action plan starter template, vendor/supply-chain climate exposure questionnaire, cost-per-outcome/SROI quick calculator.

## 4. Cross-population mechanics, how this actually compounds

A resource library that doesn't feed the rest of the content system is just an archive. The point is the loop:

- **LinkedIn → Guide:** every post that references a term, framework, or statistic links to its Guide entry instead of re-explaining it inline. This shortens posts and gives every post a permanent home to send traffic to.
- **Newsletter → Guide:** a recurring "new in the Guide" section repurposes whatever shipped that week, one glossary term, one template, one comparison page, into a two-line newsletter item. Near-zero additional writing cost.
- **Workshops → Guide:** the Guide becomes the standard leave-behind. Instead of a slide deck nobody reopens, attendees get a URL they can actually search and return to.
- **Client conversations → Guide:** "we've written up the plain version of this on our resource library" is a credibility move that costs nothing and never exposes proprietary methodology, the CAST scoring weights stay locked; the definitions and context around them don't need to be.
- **Guide → CAST/CSR-D:** every role guide, sector playbook, and template ends on a soft CTA into the relevant service, matched to what the reader just demonstrated interest in. The materiality tracker built in this pass ends on exactly this pattern, a "scored mostly High?" prompt into CAST.
- **Guide → Guide:** internal linking in a hub-and-spoke pattern (category hub → individual entries → related entries) is what actually builds topical authority with search engines, not the number of pages alone.

## 5. SEO, AEO and GEO, why this is built the way it is

- **Structured data on every page type.** `DefinedTermSet`/`DefinedTerm` schema on the glossary, `CollectionPage` on the hub, and `FAQPage` schema once the mythbuster hub exists. This is what lets an AI answer engine or a Google featured snippet lift a definition cleanly instead of guessing at it.
- **Short, factual, sourced content wins the AI-citation game.** Long, hedged prose doesn't get quoted by an answer engine, a tight, well-sourced paragraph does. Every glossary entry in this build is written to that constraint deliberately.
- **Internal linking depth over page count.** Ten well-linked pages beat fifty orphaned ones. Every page built in this pass links to at least two others in the Guide.
- **Every India-specific claim carries a verification note.** Regulatory facts decay. The Regulations Library page states its verification cadence explicitly (checked against SEBI/MoEFCC, re-verified quarterly) rather than implying permanence it can't promise.

## 6. Build roadmap, realistic phasing against solo capacity

This is where the honest note lives: a resource library this size is a multi-year asset, not a launch-week deliverable, and you are one person mid-outreach sprint. The structure below is built so each phase can go live the moment it's ready, without waiting for the next phase to be finished.

**Phase 0, this pass (complete):**
Information architecture for all fourteen categories, hub page, and one fully working example of each core content type (glossary, comparison page, role guide, downloadable template) so the shape exists and can be filled incrementally without redesigning anything.

**Phase 1, alongside current outreach sprint (thin slices, ~2–3 hours/week):**
- Glossary: expand from 12 to 30 core terms
- One additional sector playbook (pharma)
- FAQ/mythbuster hub launched with 8–10 entries, highest ROI per hour of any category here
- One additional template (BRSR Core readiness self-assessment)

**Phase 2, post first lighthouse logos:**
- Remaining role-based guides (Sustainability Lead, HR/Duty-of-Care, CFO, Operations, Pharma Access)
- Business decision guides
- Comparison/"X vs Y" page cluster
- Remaining sector playbooks

**Phase 3, once traffic data justifies the build cost:**
- India Policy & Deadline Tracker (needs a light content-ops habit to stay current, don't start this until you can commit to weekly maintenance)
- Climate-Health & Equity evidence library
- Case study/pattern library
- Calculators and interactive tools
- Reading list / tool directory

**Do not build Phase 3 before Phase 1 is live and getting traffic.** The Policy Tracker in particular is a maintenance commitment, not a one-time build, starting it before you have a rhythm for the rest of the Guide is exactly the "asset production substituting for market contact" pattern your own memory flags. Ship Phase 1 first, watch what actually gets read, then decide what Phase 2/3 priority order really is based on data instead of on this document's guesses.

## 7. Governance and voice

- All copy in the Guide follows `voice_sheet.md` exactly, British English, no em dashes, no banned words, prose-first. This document and every page built in this pass were checked against it.
- The Guide is signed by Abhijith Magal L as maintainer, not by "the Syntropy Earth team", consistent with the Year 1 personal-brand-builds-company-brand sequencing already locked in memory.
- Nothing proprietary lives here. If a piece of content would reveal the CAST scoring methodology, the specific weighting logic, or client-identifiable detail, it doesn't go in the Guide, it stays in the paid engagement. The test: could a competitor read this page and rebuild your diagnostic from it? If yes, it's too much detail for the Guide.
- Every factual claim about a regulation, a standard, or a statistic must be independently verifiable at the time of writing. Where a number could be stale by the time someone reads it (regulatory thresholds, filing dates), the page states its own verification cadence rather than presenting the number as permanent.

## 8. Technical approach

The Guide is static HTML/CSS with no build step, using the exact CSS custom properties and font stack from `syntropy-earth-style-guide-v2.md` (Cormorant Garamond, DM Sans, Space Mono; Premium Heritage palette), now inlined into each page rather than linked, so nothing depends on a relative asset path surviving a folder move. This matches the actual live stack, hand-coded HTML/CSS/JS pushed via GitHub to Netlify, no Framer or any other site builder anywhere in the pipeline.

Structured content (glossary entries, templates, role guides) is staged in `syntropy-guide-cms-template.xlsx`, built for upload into Google Sheets, see `analytics-and-gtm-setup.md` and the Content tab's column reference for the fields (`category`, `title`, `slug`, `summary`, `body`, `related_entries`, `date_published`, `date_verified`, `status`). This is a staging and editorial-tracking layer, not a build pipeline, entries get copied from the sheet into the HTML by hand until volume justifies a small automation script, per Section 6's phasing discipline.
