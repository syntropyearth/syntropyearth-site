Climate-Health Materiality Tracker

Score which climate risks actually threaten a health or access programme, across four dimensions, before you commit budget to fixing any of them. Free. No email required.

Download the .xlsx

GA4 also logs this automatically as file_download via Enhanced Measurement — this button additionally fires a named template_download event so this specific asset is a one-click filter in reporting.

What's inside

Two tabs. An instructions sheet explaining the scoring logic, and a working tracker with four sample risk entries already scored so you can see the format in use. The tracker scores every risk on Likelihood x Severity (1 to 5 each), auto-calculates a priority tier, and summarises how many High, Medium and Low priority risks you're carrying.

The four dimensions it scores

Geographic & Physical Access

Can people physically reach the service when climate stress hits — flooded roads, storm damage, seasonal isolation.

Supply Chain Integrity

Does the cold chain, the drug supply, or the equipment stay intact under heat, power loss, or transport disruption.

Workforce Availability

Can staff safely work and travel during heat advisories, flooding, or other extreme weather days.

Financial & Demand Continuity

Does patient affordability or programme demand hold up after a climate shock hits the local economy.

Where this fits

This is a self-assessment starting point, not a full diagnostic. It's the same four dimensions our CAST engagement scores in depth, with external validation and a weighted methodology, across a complete climate-health year. Use this tracker to find out whether that fuller diagnostic is worth commissioning.

Scored mostly High or Medium?

That's usually the signal a full CAST diagnostic will pay for itself.
