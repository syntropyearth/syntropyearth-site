BRSR, Business Responsibility & Sustainability Report

India's mandatory ESG disclosure format, decoded structurally: the nine principles, how BRSR Core assurance works, and where climate-health data fits into it.

What it actually is

The Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report is the mechanism SEBI uses to make India's largest listed companies disclose their environmental, social and governance performance in a structured, comparable format, rather than leaving it to whatever a company chooses to put in a voluntary annual report. It replaced the older, thinner Business Responsibility Report starting FY 2022-23, and applies to the top 1,000 listed companies by market capitalisation.

BRSR is built around nine principles, drawn from India's National Guidelines on Responsible Business Conduct (NGRBC). Each principle covers a distinct area of responsible business conduct, and every applicable company reports against all nine, every year.

The nine principles, at a glance

P1

Ethics, transparency, accountability

Governance conduct and anti-corruption.

P2

Safe, sustainable products

Product life cycle responsibility.

P3

Employee wellbeing

Workforce welfare, safety, and rights.

P4

Stakeholder interests

Responsiveness to all stakeholders, including the vulnerable.

P5

Human rights

Respect for and promotion of human rights.

P6

Environment

Environmental protection and restoration.

P7

Public and regulatory policy

Responsible engagement in policy advocacy.

P8

Inclusive growth

Equitable development, especially for underserved groups.

P9

Customer value

Responsible engagement with consumers.

How BRSR Core changes the stakes

BRSR Core is a smaller subset of key performance indicators, pulled from across the nine principles, that carries a phased external assurance requirement, meaning an independent auditor has to verify the numbers, not just the company itself. SEBI introduced this in 2023 specifically because self-reported ESG data, without any external check, was proving unreliable at scale. Assurance started with India's largest listed companies and is expanding in phases to eventually cover the full top 1,000.

The reporting process, in sequence

Step 1

Company collects data against all nine principles across the reporting year, environmental metrics, workforce data, governance disclosures.

↓
Step 2

BRSR Core KPIs, where applicable, go through independent external assurance before filing.

↓
Step 3

Full BRSR is filed as part of the company's annual report to SEBI and made public.

↓
Step 4

Investors, regulators, and the public can access and compare disclosures across companies in a standard format.

Where climate-health work fits

A climate-health diagnostic, scoring how climate stress affects access, supply chain, and workforce, produces exactly the kind of structured, verifiable data BRSR Core assurance is starting to demand, particularly under Principle 3 (employee wellbeing) and Principle 6 (environment). This is one of the few CSR-adjacent workstreams that directly strengthens a compliance filing rather than sitting separately from it.

How BRSR relates to other frameworks

GRI, voluntary global baseline TCFD → ISSB, climate financial risk SDGs, outcome vocabulary

Companies that report BRSR often also reference GRI internationally, since GRI is the more globally recognised vocabulary, but the two are not interchangeable. BRSR is a specific, mandatory Indian legal filing; GRI is a voluntary global standard a company can choose to also align with.

Abbreviations used on this page

BRSR: Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report · BRSR Core: assured KPI subset of BRSR · SEBI: Securities and Exchange Board of India · CSR: Corporate Social Responsibility · NGRBC: National Guidelines on Responsible Business Conduct
