Your access data is an average. Averages hide where the programme is already failing.

Why this is on your desk

No domestic law in your operating market may require this. The obligation comes down from a parent headquarters instead -- a global access commitment, a sustainability pledge, a target with a reporting date already attached. The local team's job is turning that promise into a measured outcome headquarters can stand behind.

You're already spending, and already reporting. The question now is whether it survives assurance -- BRSR Core assurance extending across India's largest listed companies, and equivalent scrutiny wherever your group discloses. Most access programmes were built to show reach, not to survive an assurer's question.

This one runs through your own field force -- medical representatives, community health workers, cold-chain and distribution staff working through heat, flood and storm. That exposure is becoming a named line of employer responsibility, and the answer needs to be designed, not improvised the season it's tested.
