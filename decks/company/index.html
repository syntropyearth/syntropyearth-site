02 · THE NAME

Syntropy is the inverse of entropy.

Entropy is the tendency of systems toward disorder -- energy dissipating, structure breaking down, connection coming apart. Syntropy is the opposite pull: the tendency toward order, connection and increasing organisation.

Climate disruption to delivery is today scattered, unmeasured and reactive -- entropy, in commercial form. The company exists to build order and defensible measurement into that domain. The name is the method.

03 · WHY CLIMATE, WHY NOW

Disclosure is moving from voluntary to audited.

What is shifting

Climate accountability is moving from reputational to mandated and audited, jurisdiction by jurisdiction. Europe's disclosure regime is in force. ISSB-aligned standards are being adopted across the UK, Asia-Pacific and beyond. India is extending third-party assurance across its largest listed companies. The direction of travel is one way, everywhere.

The window

This is a three-to-five-year inflection, not a distant one. What is voluntary today becomes disclosed, audited and board-scrutinised tomorrow. Organisations that build measurement capability now set the terms of their own story. The rest will build it later, under audit pressure, at audit prices.

Climate is not a new department to fund. It is a lens that changes what resilient and responsible already mean, inside what a company already reports and already spends.

Next · The method, named
05 · THE FLAGSHIP · CLIMATE ACCESS STRESS TEST

CAST tests whether your reporting would survive scrutiny.

Every organisation already reports something -- a regulatory filing, a GRI-aligned report, an annual sustainability disclosure. CAST maps that reporting against actual resilience across four dimensions, over a full climate year -- monsoon, heat, flood, storm, whatever the disruption season looks like where you operate -- and scores what holds.

01

Geographic and physical exposure

Can sites, facilities and delivery points keep functioning through flood, heat and storm -- or does the map itself become the failure point?

02

Supply chain and operational continuity

Do supply, logistics and last-mile delivery hold through the disruption season, or quietly fail at the final kilometre?

03

Workforce availability

Can the people the operation depends on keep working, keep travelling and keep showing up through climate stress?

04

Financial and demand continuity

Do demand, payment and uptake hold when climate stress reaches household income -- or does the customer disappear with the season?

The method is the same whether the organisation delivers a drug, a loan, a service contract or a product. The four dimensions describe continuity of what is delivered to the people who depend on it.
